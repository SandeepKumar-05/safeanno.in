# വെള്ളം കേറിയോ?

**"Has the water risen?"** — that's what every Keralite asks their neighbour the moment it starts raining heavily.

This is a free, open web app for Kerala that lets anyone report a flood, landslide, storm, or road block in real time — and instantly warns travelers whose route passes through that area. No login. No app download. Just open the website and report.

---

## The idea

Every monsoon Kerala loses lives not because people didn't know a disaster was coming — but because the person *driving toward it* had no way of knowing the person *already there* had seen it.

A nurse riding to the hospital at 5am doesn't know the bypass flooded an hour ago. A family driving to Munnar doesn't know the mountain road washed out overnight. A fisherman heading out to sea doesn't know the swell turned dangerous at dawn.

This app is the missing link between the person who sees it and the person who needs to know.

---

## How it works

Anyone who sees a flooded road, a landslide, a fallen tree, or any danger taps the map, drops a pin on the exact location, types a short message in Malayalam or English, and hits send. That report appears live on the map for everyone. If you have saved your travel route, you get an instant notification on your phone the moment a report comes in anywhere along your way.

No account is needed at any point. The whole thing works in a browser.

---

## What it covers

Flood, landslide, storm, lightning, high tide, road block, tree fall, and power failure — the eight most common monsoon disasters in Kerala. Reports show up colour-coded by severity so you can see at a glance what is serious and what is minor.

---

## Built for Kerala specifically

The entire interface is in Malayalam first. All 14 districts are tracked. The map is centred on Kerala. The emergency numbers shown are KSDMA, NDRF, Kerala Police, and Kerala Fire. The app was designed around how people in Kerala actually use their phones during a crisis — quickly, on cheap Android devices, often on a shaky 4G connection.

---

## Technology

React for the frontend. Supabase for the database and real-time updates. Leaflet and OpenStreetMap for the map. Web Push for browser notifications. The whole backend runs on Supabase's free tier. There is no server to maintain.
